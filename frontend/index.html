How it works

Watch a bot trade — and finally understand how.

Most trading bots are black boxes that just show a number going up or down. AlphaGlyph narrates the entire decision so you actually learn.

1 · It reads the market

Every few minutes it measures trend strength and volatility, classifies the market into one of four regimes, and picks the strategy that fits.

2 · It trades on its own

It generates signals, sizes each position by your edge (Kelly), sets a trailing stop, and executes — you never place an order yourself.

3 · It shows its work

Every trade comes with a plain-English reason — what the strategy saw, in what market, and why it bought or sold. No jargon, no black box.

# Every cycle, for each stock it watches:
regime   = detect_regime(SPY)        # trend + volatility
strategy = pick_for(regime, risk)    # adaptive choice

if signal == BUY and risk_ok(ticker):
    buy(ticker, kelly_size(edge))
    explain(why(strategy, regime))

Under the hood

Built like a real quant desk — open for you to see.

Every indicator, model, and statistical test is implemented from first principles. No black-box libraries.

An ML transformer you can watch think

A multi-modal transformer forecasts a full return distribution and direction probability per stock — and the dashboard shows it live, not just a buy/sell flag.

Run your own — no code

Pick a strategy, your stocks, and your risk. It paper-trades on real prices instantly, right in your browser. No programming, no account.

Adaptive regime detection

ADX, Bollinger Band Width, and realized volatility classify the market into four regimes and automatically switch the strategy to match.

Trailing stops & Kelly sizing

A trailing stop locks in gains as price climbs; the Kelly Criterion sizes each position by your historical edge, not a fixed percentage.

Strategy leaderboard

Run every strategy on the same stocks and period, then rank them head-to-head by return, Sharpe, drawdown, and more.

Honest by design

Monte Carlo, the Deflated Sharpe Ratio, and Fama-French factor attribution tell you whether an edge is genuine — or just luck.
